BSides Kraków 2026: the essential event guide

23 July 2026 · 6 min read · By the BSides Kraków Team

BSides Kraków returns on Saturday, 26 September 2026, bringing the cybersecurity community together at AGH University in Kraków, Poland. Here is the practical information you need to plan your day and make the most of it.

What is BSides Kraków?

Security BSides events are community-led gatherings created to make information security knowledge more open, practical and collaborative. BSides Kraków brings that spirit to one of Poland’s strongest technology hubs. The event welcomes security practitioners, software developers, students, researchers, leaders and anyone genuinely curious about how security works in the real world.

This is not only a sequence of presentations. Talks, hands-on activities, villages and the conversations between them all contribute to the experience. You can follow a technical theme throughout the day, explore a topic outside your usual role or simply meet people solving similar problems.

Date and venue

The 2026 conference takes place on Saturday, 26 September 2026 at the Faculty of Computer Science, AGH University of Krakow, Building D-17.

Venue: AGH University, Faculty of Computer Science, Building D-17
Address: Kawiory 21, 30-055 Kraków, Poland
Date: Saturday, 26 September 2026
Format: In-person cybersecurity conference

Most conference activity is located on the ground floor, including the villages, sponsors and Tracks 1 and 3. Track 2 is on the first floor. Review the venue guide and floor maps before you travel, especially if you want to move quickly between sessions.

What to expect

Technical talks

The programme brings together perspectives from across cybersecurity. Depending on the final agenda, your day might include application security, cloud and infrastructure, offensive and defensive security, security research, incident response, privacy or the human side of building safer systems. Check the conference schedule for the latest published session details.

Security villages and hands-on learning

Villages make it possible to learn by doing. They are also an easy place to ask questions, compare approaches and meet people without the formality of a lecture room. If a topic is unfamiliar, that is a good reason to stop by—community events are built for exploration.

Training and deeper practice

Training sessions offer space for more focused learning. Review the training information in advance because individual sessions may have their own requirements or capacity.

People worth meeting

The audience is part of the programme. Expect a mix of experienced practitioners, first-time attendees, students, speakers, volunteers and community organisers. Bring questions, share what you are working on and be open about what you are still learning.

Tickets and registration

General admission is free, with a donation option available for people who want to support the event. Capacity is limited, so reserve your ticket through Eventbrite. If your plans change, release your place so another community member can attend.

How to plan a strong conference day

  1. Choose two or three priority sessions. Leave room for unexpected conversations and hands-on activities.
  2. Read the venue map. Knowing which floor hosts each track saves time between talks.
  3. Arrive with questions. A specific challenge from your work or studies is an excellent conversation starter.
  4. Meet one new person. You do not need to network with everyone for the day to be valuable.
  5. Capture one action. Write down a tool to test, an idea to discuss with your team or a person to follow up with.

Who should attend?

BSides Kraków is relevant whether you work full-time in security or encounter security as part of another role. The event is designed for application and cloud security professionals, penetration testers, defenders, developers, platform engineers, students, researchers, security champions and technology leaders. Curiosity matters more than job title.

See you in Kraków

Come for the technical programme, but make space for the community around it. The best conference outcome is often a combination of one useful idea, one honest conversation and one new direction to explore.
