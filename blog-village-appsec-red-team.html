AppSec & Red Team Village: build, break, learn

11 June 2026 · 6 min read · By the BSides Kraków Team

Secure software improves when the people who build it understand how it fails—and when offensive practitioners understand how modern systems are designed and delivered. This village brings both perspectives into the same room.

The theme: feedback between builders and breakers

The AppSec & Red Team Village focuses on practical application security and offensive tradecraft. It is a place to explore how weaknesses emerge in code, APIs, identities, cloud services and software supply chains, then connect those lessons to better design and defence.

The aim is not to celebrate exploitation for its own sake. It is to make attack paths understandable, reproducible and useful to the people responsible for reducing risk.

Core question: how can offensive insight become faster, clearer security feedback for developers and defenders?

Topics that belong in the conversation

  • Web, API and mobile application testing.
  • Source-code review and secure design.
  • Identity attacks, lateral movement and adversary simulation.
  • Cloud, container and CI/CD attack paths.
  • Detection engineering and purple-team feedback.
  • Vulnerability research, disclosure and responsible remediation.
  • Tools and automation that help without hiding the underlying technique.

Who should visit?

Application security engineers, penetration testers, red teamers, developers, security champions, detection engineers and curious students will all find useful entry points. Beginners can observe a technique and ask why it works; experienced practitioners can compare workflows, tools and tradecraft.

How to get the most from it

Bring a laptop only when an activity requests one, and never test a system without explicit permission. Bring a question from your own environment: a noisy finding, a difficult threat model, an attack path you cannot explain or a remediation that repeatedly stalls.

Village activities and timings may change as the programme develops. Review the official schedule before the conference.
