Cyber Games Village: play to learn, learn to defend

24 June 2026 · 5 min read · By the BSides Kraków Team

Security concepts become easier to remember when people can test them together. The Cyber Games Village turns abstract threats and defensive choices into shared, hands-on experiences.

The theme: learning through play

The village uses games and challenges to create conversation, reveal assumptions and practise decision-making. Activities can make topics such as threat modelling, incident response, privacy and security culture approachable without making them simplistic.

A game provides a common system, a set of constraints and permission to experiment. That makes it easier for people from different roles to explain how they see risk and learn from the choices of others.

Core idea: play is not a break from serious security work—it is a structured way to practise it.

What you may explore

  • Threat-modelling games that help teams discover design risks.
  • Incident scenarios that test communication and prioritisation.
  • Privacy exercises that reveal how data decisions affect people.
  • Collaborative challenges where every participant contributes a different perspective.
  • Friendly competition that rewards curiosity rather than prior status.

Who should join?

No specialist background is required. Developers, security professionals, product teams, students, managers and first-time attendees can all participate. Games are especially useful for mixed groups because the activity creates a shared vocabulary.

How to participate well

Explain your reasoning, invite quieter participants into the discussion and treat the result as feedback rather than a score. The most useful moment is often not winning—it is discovering why another person made a completely different decision.

CyberSec Games supports the event as a collaborator and brings a play-based approach to security learning. Check the official schedule for the final village activities and timings.
